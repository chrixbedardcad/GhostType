Step 1: Accessibility

GhostSpell needs Accessibility permission to select and replace text in any app.
Accessibility Not granted
This lets GhostSpell simulate keyboard shortcuts (Cmd+A, Cmd+C, Cmd+V) to capture and replace text.
Find GhostSpell in the list and toggle it ON. The status above updates automatically.
After an update? Toggle OFF, then ON again. If that doesn't work, click the − button to remove GhostSpell, then click + to re-add it.
Required: GhostSpell cannot work without Accessibility permission. It is needed to capture and paste text.

Step 2: Input Monitoring

GhostSpell needs Input Monitoring to listen for your hotkey (Ctrl+G).
Input Monitoring Not granted
This lets GhostSpell detect when you press the hotkey (Ctrl+G), so it knows when to process your text.
How to add GhostSpell:
1. Click the + button at the bottom of the list
2. Navigate to Applications → GhostSpell
3. Click Open to add it
4. Make sure the toggle next to GhostSpell is ON
Note: After granting this permission, GhostSpell needs to restart so macOS can activate the hotkey listener. Click Next to proceed to restart.
Required: Without Input Monitoring, the hotkey will not work. GhostSpell cannot process text without it.
🔄

Restart Required

macOS requires GhostSpell to restart for the new permissions to take effect.

The app will close and reopen automatically. If it doesn't, open GhostSpell from your Applications folder or Spotlight.
